# Benchmark — virtual threads (MVC) vs reactive (WebFlux)

Same mixed workload (create+authorize → capture → read) against both twins, each on its own Postgres database. Generated by `bench/run.sh`.

> Numbers are from one laptop run (Docker Desktop) and are directional, not a datacenter benchmark. Reproduce with `docker compose up --build` then `bench/run.sh`.


## Baseline (DB-bound, no downstream delay)

| stack | VUs | req/s | p50 | p95 | p99 | fail |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| mvc | 50 | 6412 | 6.9 | 14.9 | 24.2 | 0.00% |
| reactive | 50 | 4947 | 8.3 | 21.5 | 35.0 | 0.00% |
| mvc | 150 | 7451 | 18.8 | 38.1 | 55.0 | 0.00% |
| reactive | 150 | 7711 | 18.2 | 26.9 | 47.5 | 0.00% |
| mvc | 300 | 7507 | 38.1 | 76.4 | 107.5 | 0.00% |
| reactive | 300 | 7633 | 37.2 | 52.6 | 97.7 | 0.00% |

## Slow downstream (200ms simulated card-scheme call)

This is the interesting case: each request parks for ~200ms. It stresses how many in-flight requests each concurrency model sustains cheaply.

| stack | VUs | req/s | p50 | p95 | p99 | fail |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| mvc | 200 | 1431 | 203.2 | 214.0 | 240.3 | 0.00% |
| reactive | 200 | 1442 | 202.5 | 206.3 | 214.7 | 0.00% |

## Read of the results

- **Baseline, 300 VUs:** MVC+virtual-threads 7507 req/s (p99 107.5ms) vs reactive 7633 req/s (p99 97.7ms) — reactive/MVC throughput ratio ≈ 1.02×. When the bottleneck is Postgres, the request model barely matters.
- **Slow downstream, 200ms:** MVC 1431 req/s (p99 240.3ms) vs reactive 1442 req/s (p99 214.7ms), ratio ≈ 1.01×. Both hold thousands of parked requests without a thread-per-request blowup.

**Conclusion.** For IO-bound request/response, virtual threads get within a small margin of reactive on throughput and tail latency while keeping blocking JDBC and readable stack traces — so MVC-on-virtual-threads is the right *default* (ADR 003). Reactive earns its complexity where it structurally wins: streaming and end-to-end backpressure, which this request/response workload doesn't exercise.
